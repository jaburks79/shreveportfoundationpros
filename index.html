Why Shreveport Has More Foundation Problems Than Most U.S. Cities

The Kessler clay soil common throughout Caddo and Bossier Parishes is among the most expansive soil types in Louisiana. When Shreveport gets its typical 50+ inches of annual rainfall, that clay swells dramatically. During our dry stretches — especially the summer months — it shrinks and pulls away from your foundation. This constant expand-and-contract cycle is the primary reason Shreveport homeowners deal with foundation movement that homeowners in other states rarely experience. Pier and beam homes built before 1980 are especially vulnerable, as the original wood beams were not treated for Louisiana's humidity levels.

Warning Signs

Is Your Shreveport Home Showing These Signs?

Shreveport's clay soil moves constantly. These are the most common warning signs we see in homes across Caddo and Bossier Parish. If you're seeing any of these, call us for a free inspection before the problem worsens.

⚠️

Cracks in Drywall or Brick

Diagonal cracks from door corners or stair-step cracks in brick veneer are classic signs of differential foundation settlement in clay soil.

⚠️

Sticking Doors or Windows

When your foundation shifts, door frames go out of square. Doors that suddenly won't latch or windows that won't open freely are common early signals.

⚠️

Sloping or Bouncy Floors

If a marble rolls toward one side of your living room, or if floors feel springy underfoot, your pier and beam system likely needs attention.

⚠️

Gaps Around Windows or Doors

Visible gaps between window frames and walls, or between exterior doors and their frames, indicate your home's structure is no longer square.

⚠️

Visible Foundation Cracks

Horizontal cracks in foundation walls are serious. Vertical hairline cracks may be minor. We'll tell you exactly which is which — for free.

⚠️

Musty Crawl Space Odors

A damp or musty smell coming from floor vents often means your crawl space is holding moisture — which leads to wood rot and foundation instability.

Common Questions About Foundation Repair in Shreveport

How much does foundation repair cost in Shreveport, LA? ▼
Foundation repair costs in Shreveport typically range from $3,500 to $18,000 depending on the severity of the problem, the number of piers required, and whether drainage corrections are needed. Pier and beam repairs on older homes often run $4,000–$9,000. Slab foundation repair using steel push piers typically starts around $5,500. We provide free written estimates so you know exactly what you're paying before we start.
Why does Shreveport have so many foundation problems? ▼
Caddo and Bossier Parish sit on Kessler clay — one of the most expansive soil types in Louisiana. This clay absorbs water and swells significantly during Shreveport's rainy periods (we average 50+ inches annually), then shrinks and cracks during dry summers. This constant movement is the primary driver of foundation problems in the area. Homes built before 1980 on pier and beam foundations are especially vulnerable because the original wood beams were not treated for Louisiana's humidity levels.
Is foundation repair covered by homeowner's insurance in Louisiana? ▼
Most standard Louisiana homeowner's insurance policies do not cover foundation repair caused by soil movement, settlement, or shrinkage — which covers the vast majority of Shreveport foundation issues. However, if the damage was caused by a sudden event like a plumbing leak under the slab, that may be covered. We recommend checking your policy directly, and we're happy to provide documentation needed for any insurance claim you pursue.
How long does foundation repair take in Shreveport? ▼
Most residential foundation repairs in Shreveport take 1–3 days. Pier installation jobs typically complete in 1–2 days depending on the number of piers. Pier and beam repairs involving beam replacement may take 2–3 days. Crawl space encapsulation is usually a full day. We give you an accurate timeline in your written estimate so you can plan accordingly.
Do I need to leave my home during foundation repair? ▼
In most cases, no. Our crews work primarily from the exterior of your home for slab pier installation, and the process is not disruptive enough to require you to vacate. For crawl space repairs, you'll want to stay out of the affected area while crews are working but can remain in the rest of the house. We'll discuss any specific requirements during your estimate.
What is a lifetime transferable warranty on foundation repair? ▼
Our lifetime transferable warranty means the warranty stays with the property, not the owner. If you sell your Shreveport home, the warranty transfers to the new owners — which can be a meaningful selling point. The warranty covers the structural integrity of the repair work we performed. We'll walk you through the specific terms at completion and register the warranty for you.
